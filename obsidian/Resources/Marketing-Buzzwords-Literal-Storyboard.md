# "If Marketing Buzzwords Were Literal" - 60s Storyboard

## Overall Style
- **Language**: English (for American Hackathon)
- **Pace**: 5-6 seconds per gag, rapid cuts, no breathing room
- **Tone**: Deadpan serious - all characters with dead expressions, like this is completely normal
- **BGM**: Serious corporate training video music throughout, twist at the end

---

## [0-3s] Title Card
- **Visual**: Standard corporate title card, blue background, white text, very "serious"
- **Text on screen**: "If Marketing Buzzwords Were Literal"
- **Audio**: Light corporate ukulele BGM

---

## [3-10s] Gag 1: "We need more EXPOSURE"

### [3-5s]
- **Visual**: Boss in conference room speaking, subtitle pops up
- **Dialogue**: Boss (deadpan): "We need more exposure."

### [5-10s]
- **Visual**: Marketing manager nods, walks out of office, starts undressing on the street. Passersby look back in shock. His expression is completely calm, like doing something totally normal.
- **Audio**: Ambient street sounds, some gasps
- **Key humor**: Deadpan expression + absurd behavior contrast

---

## [10-16s] Gag 2: "Build a sales FUNNEL"

### [10-12s]
- **Visual**: Same boss
- **Dialogue**: "Let's build a sales funnel."

### [12-16s]
- **Visual**: Cut to: A person in office seriously welding a huge metal funnel, pouring cash into it. Coworker walks by drinking coffee, completely unbothered.
- **Audio**: Metal clanking + cash rustling sounds
- **Key humor**: Taking "funnel" literally, coworker normalcy

---

## [16-22s] Gag 3: "Let's do some COLD CALLING"

### [16-18s]
- **Visual**: Boss
- **Dialogue**: "Time for some cold calling."

### [18-22s]
- **Visual**: Employee wearing full North Face, scarf, snow boots, standing in blizzard making a call. Lips frozen purple, hands shaking trying to press the number. On the call, shivering: "H-h-hi, is this a g-good time?"
- **Audio**: Wind howling, shivering voice
- **Key humor**: Every American knows the pain of cold calling, literal translation makes pain absurd

---

## [22-28s] Gag 4: "We need more ENGAGEMENT"

### [22-24s]
- **Visual**: Boss
- **Dialogue**: "Our engagement numbers are low."

### [24-28s]
- **Visual**: Marketing manager walks up to client, gets down on one knee, pulls out diamond ring: "Will you… engage with our Q3 content strategy?"
- **Client**: Shocked/touched to tears
- **Key humor**: Engagement = proposal, mixing marketing language with proposal scripts

---

## [28-34s] Gag 5: "Let's CIRCLE BACK"

### [28-30s]
- **Visual**: Boss
- **Dialogue**: "Let's circle back on this."

### [30-34s]
- **Visual**: Group of suited people in conference room holding hands running in circles like elementary school kids playing a game. One person gasping: "Are we… are we done circling?" Another: "No. We circle BACK." They start running the opposite direction.
- **Audio**: Heavy breathing, sneakers squeaking
- **Key humor**: "Circle back" is the most complained-about corporate buzzword, literal version is extremely absurd

---

## [34-40s] Gag 6: "We need to MOVE THE NEEDLE"

### [34-36s]
- **Visual**: Boss
- **Dialogue**: "We really need to move the needle."

### [36-40s]
- **Visual**: Employee sweating, pushing a HUGE needle (like a monument), pushes for ages and it moves 1mm. Turns and yells: "I MOVED IT!" Boss yells from distance: "Not enough!"
- **Audio**: Straining sounds, heavy breathing
- **Key humor**: Every American worker has heard this phrase

---

## [40-46s] Gag 7: "Think OUTSIDE THE BOX"

### [40-42s]
- **Visual**: Boss
- **Dialogue**: "I need you to think outside the box."

### [42-46s]
- **Visual**: Employee actually sitting inside a big cardboard box in the office. When he hears the instruction, he struggles to crawl OUT, stands outside the box, stares at the air, looks confused: "...now what?"
- **Key humor**: Most mocked corporate cliché in America

---

## [46-52s] Gag 8 (Climax): "Let's do GUERRILLA MARKETING"

### [46-48s]
- **Visual**: Boss
- **Dialogue**: "It's time for guerrilla marketing."

### [48-52s]
- **Visual**: A REAL gorilla (person in gorilla suit) wearing suit and tie, handing out flyers on the street. People run away in fear. The gorilla chases people yelling: "DO YOU HAVE A CUSTOMER TO TALK ABOUT OUR PRODUCT?!"
- **Audio**: People screaming, gorilla grunting, chase sounds
- **Key humor**: Guerrilla/gorilla pun, visual shock + chasing people absurdity

---

## [52-60s] Ending - The Twist

### [52-55s]
- **Visual**: Sudden silence. One person sits at desk, tiredly rubbing temples.
- **Audio**: Complete silence for 1 second

### [55-57s]
- **Visual**: He looks up at camera, deadpan: "Marketing is easy. The hard part is explaining what you do at Thanksgiving."

### [57-60s]
- **Visual**: Black screen, white text, like real corporate slogan
- **Text**: "Marketing. We don't know what we're doing either." ™
- **Audio**: Low single piano note OR classic meme sound (Curb Your Enthusiasm theme)

## Technical Notes for Generation
- Use Seedance 2.0 for video generation
- Each gag is 5-8 seconds
- Maintain deadpan serious tone throughout
- Corporate aesthetic with absurd content
- Fast cuts between scenes
- Reference: typical American corporate training videos (think Deloitte University training videos)
- Final gag should have maximum visual impact (gorilla chase)
