## Draft

The narrative everyone's selling: "Learn AI or get left behind."

But here's what nobody says:

The people who need to hear this won't act on it.

Not because they're lazy. Because admitting they need to learn something new requires confronting a deeper truth—that they've been running on autopilot, and the track they're on is disappearing.

The real divide isn't technical skill. It's this:

When something new enters your awareness—a tool, a framework, a paradigm—your first instinct is either:

**"How does this work?"** (learner)
or
**"This is overhyped."** (resister)

Everything else follows from that micro-decision.

Learners compound. Resisters stagnate.

The cruelest part? The resister group doesn't feel behind. They're on what feels like solid ground. They're running the same race as always—except the finish line keeps moving, and nobody told them.

The solution isn't "learn prompt engineering."

It's asking yourself: When did I last learn something that made me uncomfortable?

If you can't remember—that's the gap.
